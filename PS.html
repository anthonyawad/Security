What is packet switching?

A mode of data transmission in which a message is broken into a number of parts which are sent independently, over whatever route is optimum for each packet, and reassembled at the destination.

What are types of packet switching?

Packet switching may be classified into connectionless packet switching, also known as datagram switching, and connection-oriented packet switching, also known as virtual circuit switching. ... Examples of connectionless protocols are Ethernet, Internet Protocol (IP), and the User Datagram Protocol (UDP).
